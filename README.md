Data analysis of physiotherapy patient outcomes to identify key factors influencing recovery time using Excel and Power BI.

## 🔍 Project Overview
This project analyzes physiotherapy patient data to identify key factors influencing recovery time and treatment outcomes. The goal is to uncover insights that can support data-driven clinical and operational decisions.

## 🧰 Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
- Power BI (Data Visualization & Dashboarding)

## 📁 Dataset
Synthetic dataset simulating physiotherapy patient records, including:
- Patient demographics  
- Treatment types  
- Compliance levels  
- Pain scores  
- Recovery duration  

## 📊 Key Insights
- Patients with lower compliance levels appeared to recover faster; however, this likely reflects milder conditions requiring fewer sessions rather than true treatment effectiveness.

- Stroke rehabilitation cases showed the longest recovery times, while low back pain cases recovered the fastest, aligning with expected clinical patterns.

- Exercise therapy was associated with faster recovery, while manual therapy resulted in slightly greater pain improvement, suggesting a trade-off between speed and depth of recovery.

- The relationship between number of sessions attended and recovery time was weak, indicating that session count alone is not a strong predictor of recovery outcomes.

- Greater reductions in pain scores did not consistently correspond to shorter recovery durations, highlighting that pain improvement alone may not fully capture overall recovery.

## 💡 Business Implications
- Treatment strategies should be tailored based on condition severity and patient goals rather than session frequency alone.
- Healthcare providers should consider multiple outcome measures beyond pain scores when evaluating recovery.
